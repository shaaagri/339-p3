King's Cross

Type

university assignment

Team

individual

My Role

3d modeler, environment artist

Tools

unity, adobe photoshop, adobe audition

As part of the second Unity assignment of IAT 455 course (the previous one was covered here), I loosely recreated the King's Cross railway station in London, which, among other things, is famous as Harry Potter's departure point on his way to Hogwarts.

unity
level design
c#

Process

Due to the open-ended nature of our assignments in that course, the most challenging stage was, again, coming up with the initial concept for the scene. I looked up at the brief and remembered my friend mentioning Harry Potter series in our talk. I started to think about what related to that theme can I make that meets the requirements, and landed on the King's Cross. This is mostly because modeling a railway station seemed pretty straightforward to me, compared to other landmarks from the HP universe.

To get the reference, I went to watch the scene from one of the old Harry Potter movies, where Harry goes to the Platform 9Â¾ for the first time.

From there, I was able to estimate the amount of work, which did not seem bad - the architecture of the station is quite repetitive. I went to collect more photos shot inside the King's Cross from various angles.

Not only I gathered enough material to model after, these photos were also later used to make flat textures out of their cut parts.

I began to model only to run into a frustrating challenge - Maya was allowed this time, but it refused to launch on my computer. Due to a tight deadline, I had to fall back to the Unity ProBuilder, which is far less powerful. Nonetheless, after watching LinkedIn Lynda tutorials I figured out the tools I needed, more or less. I eschewed any further planning and just dived into making the things, starting with train platforms.

Afterwards, I began to meticulously model the walls of King's Cross, borrowing textures where appropriate, or using Photoshop to make ones out of reference images. All the scales and proportions were just eyeballed.

I just duplicated this segment to get the actual wall, altering textures of some to imitate side entrances. The roof and adjacent parts were very tricky to make. I eventually got away by using ProBuilder's Arch tool, seamlessly connecting various pieces, and then duplicating everything once again.

From there, I prepared a roof texture and used the Arch tool with different parameters to insert glass panels into the roof.

I continued to fill in details, such as these platform stands and the sunny lighting.

The assignment had us integrate at least one borrowed model, and create a particle system somewhere. I unified these two requirements by placing a diesel locomotive with a thick smoke (I've done it using the Unity's Visual Effect Graph) coming from its exhaust.

The assignment also had us practice collision detectors, so I came up with this improvised scene where some clumsy porter let oranges come out of cargo boxes. If you step on them, they get squished with a sound, leaving a splash on the floor. The same happens if a cart nearby rolls over them. All of this was coded in C#.

One more requirement was to use a hinge joint somewhere, so I made this storeroom with a door on hinges that squeaks if it swings too hard (just wrote a script that checks the door's angular velocity).

I also modeled the catwalk that can be seen in the reference video - Harry walks on it with Hagrid. As the final touch, I added the actual transition to the magical Platform 9Â¾. Except there is no owl in the cart.

Outcome

The project was a solid success, especially when considering the challenges I faced and the fact I was still learning Unity. The feedback from the teaching staff was very positive - full mark.

Final Thoughts

I had a lot of fun doing this project. I still feel it was somewhat too ambitious trying to recreate the whole station, though - I ended up with just an approximation. However, I feel happy that none of the challenges I faced had become real obstacles - rather they just posited questions I had to find creative answers to.
