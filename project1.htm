A Stop on the Trip

Type

university assignment

Team

individual

My Role

environment artist

Tools

unity

For the course IAT 445 Immersive Environments in SFU, dedicated to Unity and VR/AR, I completed a medium-sized scene, driven by atmosphere and exploration.

unity
level design
lighting design
sfx

Process

This was the first major assignment of the course (2 weeks to complete), and its prompt was to create a simple 3D environment in Unity that is walkable in first-person perspective.

As it's often the case, I didn't really know where to start, so first I tried to list what are the key goals I have (required by the assignment):

  • Need to use Terrain tool (a feature in Unity for quick modeling of landscapes).
  • Need to have trees, outdoor lighting, and fog.
  • Need to have audio sources, and a few simple buildings somewhere, modeled from scratch.

Besides a couple of more small requirements, the rest was left to students (yes, a lot of creative freedom). So I tried to think what also I would like to see in this environment:

  • The use of Terrain and other assets should make total sense and feel integrated. It is easy to just check the boxes dropping things randomly, but I did not want that.
  • The scene should be interesting, captivating, inciting to explore it.
  • There should be some overall theme, or mood.

Armed with that "roadmap", I began crafting the scene. Obviously, the Terrain was the largest part of this scene and really the foundation of it, so first I tried to sculpt some hills and mountains, hoping to get a clearer picture of the scene I want in the process. Initially, I was imagining some kind of Arizona/Utah desert, with an Interstate highway cutting through it, and, maybe, a gas station.

I eschewed the assignment's recommendation of using smaller landscapes because I felt what I'm really after here is that sense of being small in larger surroundings, with tall mountains at the horizon, etc.

I decided one scene's corner will be a flat lowland and the main area where I will put the most of the required assets, and where the player is going to be spawned first. The opposite corner was turned into a supertall mountain (maxed out the Terrain's possible heights, actually), to serve as a contrast to the main area, and as the "architectural dominant" for the whole scene.

Next, I began to work out the dense forest that in my vision should have been separating the two corners. This was when I realized I want the scene to be actually set at night, allowing for a more mysterious, "what's lurking behind" feel - somewhat surreal and a bit childish even. A very long browsing process had followed, until I found a perfect skybox.

Now, having a more salient visual direction allowed me to quickly come up with ideas for the lighting and fog parts. I rearranged and fleshed out the terrain, carefully distributing trees to create a natural look. Some areas remained a desert, while others became dense, lush forests (I really wanted this variety to keep the scene interesting for exploration).

Once that was done, I only needed to flesh out the main area where the player spawns. I finely sculpted the terrain here a bit more to get a feeling of the space I wanted, and put some foliage.

I still felt very attracted to my initial idea, though, of the road stop at a gas station - so next I modeled and textured a convenience store and gas pumps in Unity ProBuilder. I decided the scene, in the end, will play out as if there was some person travelling through this zone, which got abandoned and desolated due to some slowly unfolding paranormal accident.

I deliberately chose a retro, 70s look when texturing the station, to underscore that subtle surreal touch.

The lighting also became very fitting for the mood I was after. I enhanced it further with an electronic remix of a kids song "Clouds, Like a Little White Horses" from a classic Russian cartoon, playing from an old radio. Together with the ambient wind whooshing sounds, it gave the scene more of that desolated, fragile, and a little heart-aching mood.

I also carefully selected and added ambient sounds for the forest, one can hear them upon entering the most dense area.

The woods are important for the narrative, because they are the origin of the cold that consumes the region. I was somewhat inspired by Twin Peaks that similarly employed the "evil in the woods" trope - the Glastonbury Grove, in particular. To reinforce the narrative and make the forest area more interesting, I added, as a last touch, a creepy pit with a pool that illuminates trees and emits disturbing sounds. It's' probably better to stay away from it :)

Outcome

The assignment was successful. I got the full mark of A+ and a bit of praise from the teaching assistant, who is regarded as a strong expert in the domain.

Final Thoughts

Before this assignment, I had very limited experience with Unity, so it was my first major experience with the engine. The requirement to use the built-in ProBuilder instead of familiar Maya was particularly challenging. I also had to learn a lot about the Unity basics before being able to make anything useful.

However, once I got the learning done, everything started to come together and, in the end, I enjoyed the result very much. The assignment made me prepared for the more advanced Unity work of the next three projects.
